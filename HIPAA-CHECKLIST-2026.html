HIPAA Compliance Checklist (Updated for 2026)

This checklist is compiled from HHS guidance and expert resources. It helps healthcare organizations and vendors comply with HIPAA Privacy, Security, and Breach Notification Rules. Not legal advice.

Penalties can exceed $50,000 per violationreview annually or after major changes.

1. Administrative Safeguards

  • Appoint a compliance officer & provide annual training.
  • Conduct and document annual risk assessments.
  • Implement written PHI access, use, breach, and contingency policies.
  • Sign Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with all vendors handling PHI.
  • Monitor and audit PHI access (logging, periodic review).

2. Physical Safeguards

  • Secure facilities (locks, access control, surveillance).
  • Enforce device/workstation security (auto-lock, media disposal).

3. Technical Safeguards

  • Control access to ePHI with unique IDs, RBAC, and MFA.
  • Enable audit logging and integrity controls (e.g. checksums).
  • Encrypt ePHI in transit.
  • Require authentication (passwords, biometrics).

4. Privacy Rule

  • Issue Notice of Privacy Practices to patients.
  • Manage patient rights requests promptly.
  • Apply minimum necessary standard to PHI disclosures.

5. Breach Notification

  • Document and test a breach response plan; notify affected parties/HHS if needed.
  • Submit annual breach logs to HHS (if needed).

6. Ongoing Maintenance

  • Perform and document regular audits and update policies for new tech/regulations.
  • Audit vendors/business associates regularly.
